SUTAN ALP ARSLAN

the second sultan of seljuk empire

his life

  • 1029 : date of birth
  • 1059 : succeeded his father as governor of Khorasan
  • 1063 : his uncle sultan Tughril died and designated his successor as Suleiman, Arslan's infant brother
  • 1064 : Arslan defeated Kutalmish for the throne and succeeded on 27 April 1064 as sultan of the Seljuk Empire
  • 1065 : Bagrat IV submitted to paying jizya to the Seljuks but the Georgians broke the agreement in 1065.
  • 1068 : alp arslan ivaded georgia again and captured Tbilisi after a short battle and obtained the submission of Bagrat IV
  • 1071 : he had defeated Emperor Romanos IV and took him as prisoner

"Alp Arslan's victories changed the balance in near Asia completely in favour of the Seljuq Turks and Sunni Muslims. While the Byzantine Empire was to continue for nearly four more centuries, The victory at Manzikert became so popular among the Turks that later every noble family in Anatolia claimed to have had an ancestor who had fought on that day."
